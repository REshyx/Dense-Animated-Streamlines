Abstract

Streamlines are a fundamental representation in uncertainty-aware flow visualization, especially for analyzing complex transport phenomena in transitional flow regimes, enhancing researchers' ability to identify critical structures through Lagrangian coherence analysis. However, most existing studies focus on seed point optimization and static rendering, with limited attention to dynamic visualization or efficient methods for rendering dense animated streamlines. As a result, real-time animated streamline applications remain limited. To address the challenges of real-time visualizing complex flow fields and rendering large numbers of animated streamlines with scientifically meaningful motion interpretation, we propose a fast animated streamline visualization method based on a Motion Map framework. This method, based on spacetime parallelism, demonstrates how temporal domain analysis can independently capture the continuity of streamline spatial evolution. By using GPU parallel computing, our method enables real-time visualization of hundreds of millions of dynamic streamlets, accurately representing flow velocity and direction. It significantly outperforms static approaches by offering a richer, more informative representation of fluid dynamics across large-scale datasets. Furthermore, the method enhances understanding of transitional flow regimes through dynamic motion patterns, which can be tuned via multi-parameter controls to support uncertainty visualization. We also introduce a real-time interactive platform within an XR environment, providing an immersive and intuitive tool for exploring complex flow behaviors and gaining deeper insights into fluid dynamics.

Accuracy of speed representation for adaptive RK methods

Large step size fail to converge near vortices to form vortex cores, while small step sizes generate excessive primitives that sharply increase rendering load.

Adaptive step size integration can automatically adjust step size based on truncation error, achieving both more accurate streamlines and reduced primitive count.
